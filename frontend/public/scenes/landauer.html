The heat a computation can never not make — and the version that runs cold

Erasing one bit of information costs at least kT·ln2 ≈ 2.85×10⁻²¹ J of heat dumped to the environment (300 K). This furnace counts every logically irreversible bit-erasure in a real op-DAG — AND/OR/erase destroy information; NOT/COPY/Toffoli/Fredkin do not — and renders the energy floor as a billowing thermal plume. Real gate accounting, no mocks. Then it reroutes the whole circuit through reversible gates and watches the heat collapse.

What an agent buys. LANDAUER answers a question no FLOPS counter can: not how fast a computation runs, but how much heat it is thermodynamically forced to make — the floor every cooling bill and carbon estimate sits on. ARGUS-3 calls it before committing a long multi-step job to a provider: if the lossy floor towers over the reversible bound, it knows the circuit is wasting information (and energy) it never had to, and can demand a reversible re-synthesis or price the waste into escrow. Verifiable: the op-DAG is hashed to a circuit_commitment, gate semantics are fixed, and any verifier replays the erasure count to reproduce the joule floor exactly. The floor is proven by physics, not asserted.
